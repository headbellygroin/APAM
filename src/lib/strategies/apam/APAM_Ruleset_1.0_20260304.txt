Machine‑Executable Trading Rulebook for AI Systems
1. Core Concepts and Definitions
1.1 Price Zones
Demand Zone (Support Area): A consolidation area from which price rallies strongly. Formed by Drop‑Base‑Rally (DBR) or Rally‑Base‑Rally (RBR) patterns.

Supply Zone (Resistance Area): A consolidation area from which price drops strongly. Formed by Rally‑Base‑Drop (RBD) or Drop‑Base‑Drop (DBD) patterns.

1.2 Zone Lines
Proximal Line:

Demand: Highest candle body in the base.

Supply: Lowest candle body in the base.

Use median body if outlier candles distort the zone.

Distal Line:

Demand: Lowest wick of the DBR/RBR structure.

Supply: Highest wick of the RBD/DBD structure.

Outlier wicks may be trimmed using median wick values.

1.3 Base Criteria
A valid base must include:

1–6 candles

Average wick length ≥ 2× average body size

Clear rejection move ≥ 1 ATR from the base

Optional: Volume spike confirming rejection (if volume data is available)

1.4 Trend Definitions
Uptrend: Higher highs and higher lows + EMA(20) slope > 0

Downtrend: Lower highs and lower lows + EMA(20) slope < 0

Sideways: No clear structure + ADX < 25

1.5 Momentum Position (Replaces “Curve”)
Measured on the Higher Time Frame (HTF):

High Momentum: Zone located above 80% of the swing range (Fibonacci or regression channel)

Medium Momentum: Zone located between 40–60%

Low Momentum: Zone located below 20% or above 80% without divergence

Divergence: RSI(14) divergence confirms exhaustion at extremes

2. Zone Identification Rules
2.1 Pattern Recognition
Demand: DBR or RBR

Supply: RBD or DBD

2.2 Objective Identification Steps
Scan chart for consolidation clusters (1–6 candles).

Confirm strong rejection using ATR(14):

Rejection move ≥ 1 ATR from base.

Draw proximal and distal lines using body/wick rules.

Trim outlier wicks using median wick if necessary.

Validate zone freshness (see scoring system).

2.3 Zone Validity Filters
A zone is invalid if:

It has been tested deeply (>50% retrace)

It formed during low‑liquidity periods (overnight, holidays)

It formed during major news spikes (unless filtered by median wick)

3. Trend and Momentum Rules
3.1 Trend Confirmation
EMA(20) slope threshold:

Uptrend: slope > +0.01

Downtrend: slope < –0.01

ADX filter:

Trend valid only if ADX > 25

Sideways if ADX < 25

3.2 Momentum Position
Use HTF swing high/low range

Calculate zone position as % of range

Confirm with RSI(14) divergence for counter‑trend setups

A trade must score ≥ 7/10 to be valid.

4.1 Score Interpretation
8.5–10: High‑quality setup → Limit entry at zone edge

7–8: Medium‑quality setup → Confirmation entry

<7: No trade

5. Execution Logic (Evaluate → Execute → Manage)
5.1 Evaluate
Identify HTF trend and momentum

Identify MTF zones

Score setup using 10‑point model

Reject if score < 7

Reject if:

Low liquidity

Major news event upcoming

Spread/slippage abnormally high

5.2 Execute
Entry Types:

Limit‑Edge Entry: For scores ≥ 8.5

Confirmation Entry: For scores 7–8

Trigger: candle close inside zone or break of structure

Stop‑Market Entry: For breakout confirmation setups

5.3 Manage
Move stop to breakeven after 1:1 R:R

Take partial profits:

50% at 1:1

25% at 2:1

Trail remainder using ATR(14)

Log trade details for AI learning

Stop = distal line ± 1–2× ATR(14)

Round to nearest tick

Adjust for volatility regime (high ATR → wider stops)

Max daily drawdown: 3%

Max open trades: 3

No new trades after hitting daily loss limit

Primary target: next opposing fresh zone

Minimum acceptable R:R: 2:1

Use partial exits and trailing stops

Avoid low‑liquidity sessions
