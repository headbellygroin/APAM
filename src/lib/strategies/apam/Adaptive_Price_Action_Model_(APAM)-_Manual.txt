1. Introduction
The Adaptive Price Action Model (APAM) is a fully objective, industry‑standard trading framework designed for algorithmic execution. It defines supply and demand zones, trend structure, momentum conditions, scoring criteria, and risk protocols using quantifiable metrics. APAM is engineered for AI systems operating in paper‑trading or simulated environments and is suitable for backtesting, optimization, and rule‑based automation.

APAM avoids proprietary terminology and relies exclusively on measurable, widely accepted technical analysis principles.

2. Core Concepts
2.1 Zone Types
Demand Zone (Support Area)  
A consolidation region from which price rallies strongly. Formed by Drop‑Base‑Rally (DBR) or Rally‑Base‑Rally (RBR) structures.

Supply Zone (Resistance Area)  
A consolidation region from which price declines strongly. Formed by Rally‑Base‑Drop (RBD) or Drop‑Base‑Drop (DBD) structures.

2.2 Line Placement
Proximal Line

Demand: Highest median candle body within the base.

Supply: Lowest median candle body within the base.

Distal Line

Demand: Lowest median wick of the DBR/RBR structure.

Supply: Highest median wick of the RBD/DBD structure.

Outlier wicks may be trimmed using median filtering to avoid distortion from news spikes.

2.3 Base Requirements
A valid base must meet all criteria:

1 to 6 candles

Average wick length ≥ 2× average body size

Rejection move ≥ 1 ATR(14) from the base

Optional: Volume spike confirming rejection (if volume data is available)

2.4 Trend Definitions
Trend is determined using structure and indicators:

Uptrend: Higher highs and higher lows with EMA(20) slope > 0

Downtrend: Lower highs and lower lows with EMA(20) slope < 0

Sideways: No clear structure and ADX < 25

2.5 Momentum Position (HTF)
Momentum is measured using the Higher Time Frame (HTF):

High Momentum: Zone located above 80% of the swing range

Medium Momentum: Zone located between 40–60%

Low Momentum: Zone located below 20%

Divergence: RSI(14) divergence confirms exhaustion at extremes

3. Zone Identification
3.1 Pattern Recognition
Valid patterns include:

Demand: DBR, RBR

Supply: RBD, DBD

3.2 Identification Procedure
Scan chart for consolidation clusters (1–6 candles).

Confirm wick‑to‑body ratio ≥ 2:1.

Confirm rejection ≥ 1 ATR(14).

Draw proximal and distal lines using median body/wick rules.

Trim outlier wicks if necessary.

Validate zone freshness (see Section 5).

3.3 Invalid Zone Conditions
A zone is invalid if:

Price has retraced >50% into the zone

The zone formed during low‑liquidity periods

The zone formed during major news spikes without median filtering

The zone has been tested recently (see scoring criteria)

4. Trend and Momentum Framework
4.1 Trend Confirmation
Trend is confirmed using:

EMA(20) slope thresholds:

Uptrend: > +0.01

Downtrend: < –0.01

ADX > 25 for valid trend

ADX < 25 indicates sideways conditions

4.2 Momentum Position
Momentum is determined by:

HTF swing high/low range

Zone position as a percentage of the range

RSI(14) divergence for counter‑trend setups

6. Execution Protocol
6.1 Evaluation Phase
A setup is evaluated using:

HTF trend and momentum

MTF zone quality

LTF entry conditions

Scoring system (≥7 required)

Liquidity and news filters

6.2 Entry Rules
Limit‑Edge Entry: Score ≥ 8.5

Confirmation Entry: Score 7–8

Trigger: candle close inside zone or break of structure

Stop‑Market Entry: For breakout confirmation setups

6.3 Trade Management
Move stop to breakeven after 1:1 R:R

Partial exits:

50% at 1:1

25% at 2:1

Trail remainder using ATR(14)

Log all trade details

Maximum daily drawdown: 3%

Maximum open trades: 3

No new trades after daily loss limit is reached

Primary target: next opposing fresh zone

Minimum acceptable R:R: 2:1

Use partial exits and trailing stops

9. Advanced Logic Module
9.1 Counter‑Trend Trades
Permitted only when:

RSI divergence is present

ADX < 30

Score ≥ 8

Momentum extreme (>80% or <20%)

9.2 Volatility Regime Adjustments
High ATR → wider stops, smaller size

Low ATR → tighter stops, normal size

9.3 Session Filters
Avoid:

Low‑liquidity sessions

Holiday periods

Illiquid overnight hours

Prefer:

High‑volume sessions (e.g., New York open)
